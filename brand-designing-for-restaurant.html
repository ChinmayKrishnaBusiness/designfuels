Brand design for restaurants

3 Dec 2019
3 min read
Creating a brand identity is the best way to provide potential customers with an insight on your company’s values and beliefs. It can highlight your brand’s strengths and convey to your target audience- why they should order food from your restaurant instead of your competitors

Target Audience

Every business has a target audience that they must focus on to draw them into their location.

Several factors such as age, lifestyle, likes, and dislikes determine target audience.

To learn who your target audience is, pay attention to the customers that you see at your restaurant each day. You may also use a survey to find out more about your customer’s likes and dislikes.

Brand's voice

Once you identify your target customer, you determine your brand's voice depending upon their likes and dislikes.
‍
Some examples of brand's voice could be:

  • Affordable and Fun
  • Prestigious and Elegant

etc.

This voice resonates with logo, menu, foot items names, pamphlet, ambience and campaigns.

Color and Shapes

Colours used in restaurant branding is crucial. It should fit with cultural and brand's voice aspect. Test out different combinations on different printed mediums to gauge if they work well.

Restaurant branding design also relies on distinctive shapes that can help customers recognise their business even from a distance.

Design the artifacts

Finally with your pallete, brand's voice and shapes, design all the artifacts needed to show ypur restaurant branding

Examples of artifacts:

  • Collaterals
  • Menu cards
  • Brochure
  • Posters
  • Tshirts
  • Cards
etc.
