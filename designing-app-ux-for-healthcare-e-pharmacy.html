Designing app UX for healthcare (e-pharmacy)

29 Nov 2019
3 min read

Users & Problem

There are two broad categories of patients in healthcare

1. Acute Patient

2. Chronic Patient

Chronic patients regularly take medicines but acute patients stop after their illness is cured. Generally, they take medicine from the local pharmacy. For an online pharmacy, their major returning customer segment is chronic patient.
‍
Problems due to which they prefer an online pharmacy is :

  1. It’s convenient for them than to go to a local pharmacy.
  2. It has a greater discount than the local pharmacy. For a person who regularly takes medicine, budget is an issue.
  3. Chronic patients want to stock their medicine in advance.
  4. They want to read information about medicines and their usage, side effects etc.
  5. Sometimes they just want to give the prescription to e-pharmacy and they send medicines. As you know the prescription of Indian doctors is hard to read.

User experience flow

App Screens UI for each task in the flow

1. Opens the app

2. Searches by medicine name

3. Finds medicine

4. Specify Quantity

5. Adds to Cart

6. Upload Prescription

7. Select Address

8. Pay and Order

Platforms to design for

Though for Indian customer major focus of platform should be android as they are more in number but iOS users are also increasing day by day and we should design for iPhone users too.

On mobile web and desktop web, people will generally search on google for their condition and complication and end up getting search result of healthcare sites. So these healthcare sites should also design for the web to get users and pass them through their checkout funnel.

End Goal of any healthcare company should be to help their patient in a caring manner and it should reflect in their product experience
